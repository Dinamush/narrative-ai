Chapter 1: Opening

Dr. Amara Singh reviewed the patient chart and sighed. The ward was quiet except for the hum of monitors.

***

Nurse Cole entered with coffee. "You look like you haven't slept in days," Cole said.

Amara wanted to prove she was competent enough for the promotion. She knew the director doubted her judgment after the incident last month.

"I must finish these rounds," Amara replied. "People are counting on me."

Chapter 2: Crisis

The alarm sounded at midnight. Amara ran to Room 4 and found the ventilator failing.

She felt powerless for a moment — trapped between protocol and the child's shallow breath.

Amara decided to bypass the faulty valve. Cole held the tubing steady.

"That was brave," Cole whispered.

Amara realized she was no longer afraid of failing everyone. She had her own opinion despite the hospital's rigid rules.

The child stabilized. Amara smiled, exhausted but free.
