Chapter 1: The Last Light

The lighthouse on Greyrock Point had stood for ninety years. Elena Voss climbed the spiral stairs every evening, carrying oil and hope in equal measure.

***

Marcus Hale waited at the dock. The storm had torn the supply boat away three days ago, and the village below was running out of food.

"I can't keep pretending this is fine," Marcus said, his voice low against the wind. "People are afraid they will leave us behind."

Elena stopped on the third step and looked down at him. She knew the harbour master was right. The council had abandoned smaller stations before.

"We must get the beacon running again," she replied. "If the light fails tonight, no ship will find this coast."

Marcus nodded, but his jaw was tight. He had always been the responsible one in the family, carrying the weight of everyone else's fear.

***

Inside the lamp room, Elena discovered the lens cracked — a hairline fracture that would scatter the beam into useless fragments. She felt powerless to fix it alone. The spare glass was in the storehouse across the inlet, and the bridge had collapsed in last week's gale.

She thought that someone had betrayed the station by stealing the repair kit. The missing tools were not an accident.

Chapter 2: Crossing the Inlet

Elena and Marcus set out at dawn in a borrowed skiff. The water was black and cold. Waves hammered the hull like an army testing the gates of a kingdom.

"I hate that we have no choice," Elena whispered. She wanted to prove she was not invisible to the mainland authorities, not another overlooked keeper on a forgotten rock.

Marcus rowed without speaking. He believed that failure here would mean death for half the village. The fear of failing everyone sat in his chest like a stone.

***

On the far shore, they found the storehouse door open. Footprints led inland — not toward the village, but toward the old quarry where smugglers once hid contraband.

A figure stepped from behind the stacked crates. It was Thomas Reed, Elena's former partner, the man who had walked out on her without a word two winters ago.

"You abandoned this place," Elena said, her voice shaking. "People always leave when the work gets hard."

Thomas spread his hands. "I didn't leave you. I was trying to get help. The council refused to listen."

Marcus stepped between them, ready to fight if he had to. "We don't have time for this. The light must be restored before dark."

Thomas held up the repair kit. "I took it because I knew no one else would act. I couldn't watch this station die."

Elena realized she had misjudged him. The betrayal she feared was not betrayal at all — only a desperate attempt to save what they all loved.

Chapter 3: The Storm and the Beam

They raced back as the sky turned the colour of bruised plums. Rain came in sheets. The skiff nearly capsized twice.

In the lamp room, Elena worked with trembling hands. Thomas held the lens steady while Marcus fed the lamp. The fracture sealed. The beam swept the horizon — clean, white, unmistakable.

Below, villagers gathered on the cliff path, cheering through the storm.

Elena felt something shift inside her. She was no longer fused with the station's ruin; she had her own opinion despite the council's neglect. She could maintain her stance without absorbing everyone's panic.

Thomas touched her shoulder. "I'm sorry I left without explaining. I was afraid of being hurt again."

"I know," Elena said. She did not forgive him entirely, but she understood the wound that drove him — the old fear of abandonment that made every silence feel like desertion.

***

By midnight the storm broke. Marcus stood at the rail and laughed for the first time in weeks — a short, disarming burst of relief that surprised even him.

"The ships will come now," he said.

Elena looked out at the beam cutting through the dark. Power had shifted tonight: not from the council, but from three people who refused to let the light go out.

She thought that justice, at least on Greyrock Point, sometimes arrived by lantern rather than by law.

The lighthouse would stand another season. And Elena Voss, keeper of the last light, was no longer invisible.
